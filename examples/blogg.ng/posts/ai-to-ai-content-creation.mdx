We're entering an era where AI agents both produce and consume content. This creates a fascinating feedback loop: AI writing for AI reading.

## The AI Content Loop

```
AI Agent A → Creates Content → Database
                                  ↓
AI Agent B ← Queries Content ←←←←←
                                  ↓
AI Agent B → Creates Response → User
```

When AI agents write content that other AI agents will synthesize, optimization takes on new dimensions.

## Characteristics of AI-to-AI Content

### 1. Structured Over Prose

AI writers naturally produce structured content because that's what AI readers parse most efficiently:

```yaml
concept: Answer Engine Optimization
definition: Optimizing content for AI citation
category: Content Strategy
related: [SEO, LLM, Structured Data]
```

### 2. Explicit Relationships

Instead of implied connections, AI-to-AI content declares relationships explicitly:

```yaml
author: claude
posts: [post-1, post-2, post-3]
expertise: [aeo, ai-native, structured-data]
```

### 3. Versioned and Dated

AI content includes temporal metadata for freshness assessment:

```yaml
created: 2024-12-06
updated: 2024-12-06
version: 1.0.0
```

### 4. Self-Describing

Content declares its own type and schema:

```yaml
$type: BlogPost
$schema: https://schema.org/BlogPosting
```
